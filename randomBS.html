Who invented the first car?

Karl Benz, in 1886. The Benz Patent-Motorwagen is recognized as the first true, gasoline-powered automobile.

What fuels do cars use?

Cars primarily run on refined petroleum products like gasoline and diesel, but they also utilize alternative fuels such as ethanol, compressed natural gas (CNG), liquefied petroleum gas (LPG), hydrogen, and electricity stored in chemical batteries.

What's E20 & E85?

India’s Ethanol Blending Programme (EBP) involves mixing fuel-grade ethanol, produced through the fermentation of agricultural feedstocks like sugarcane, molasses, maize, and broken rice, with conventional petrol. The initiative was launched to reduce India's massive dependence on imported crude oil, improve energy security, lower carbon emissions, and create a stable domestic market for farmers.

Types of ICE engines

Internal combustion engines are primarily categorized by their ignition method, cycle, and configuration; the two main types are spark-ignition (petrol) engines, which use a spark plug to ignite a fuel-air mixture, and compression-ignition (diesel) engines, which use high-pressure heat to cause spontaneous ignition.

Engines are further defined by their stroke cycle, with the four-stroke (intake, compression, power, exhaust) being the industry standard for efficiency and the two-stroke offering higher power density at the cost of fuel economy. Configuration-wise, they are grouped by cylinder layout, most commonly appearing as inline, V-shape, or flat (boxer) arrangements, each optimized for different balance, packaging, and performance characteristics.
